About Me

I'm an Electrical Engineering student at UVic focused on electrical and software systems, machine learning, and all things system engineering. I've worked on firmware development and hardware validation for biosensing systems at Noze, and most recently built a Hardware-in-the-Loop testing infrastructure at Dometic Marine. Outside of work, I love to be outside exploring new places, whether that's skiing in the mountains or backpacking through Southeast Asia.

After graduating high school early at 17, I was deeply inspired by the adventurous, boundary-pushing lifestyles I saw online. Craving that kind of challenge, I packed up and moved out to Whistler on my own to live independently, immerse myself in the mountains, and build a life centered around active exploration.

That leap set off a transformative period where I spent a year living independently across five countries, including Indonesia, Turkey, and Vietnam. Learning to navigate unfamiliar environments and cultures entirely on my own completely reshaped how I think about problem-solving, resilience, and building technology that operates seamlessly across diverse global contexts.

A core ethos I try to live by is connection—to people, to nature, and to the work I do. I believe that the best technology is built by engineering teams who care deeply about the constraints they're navigating and the individuals they're solving them for. We are inherently social creatures, and the most impactful, high-integrity work happens when we are profoundly connected to the real-world impact of our systems.

Now, I aim to bring that same spirit of connection and adventure into my engineering work. I aspire to work with likeminded people and connect with the real-world impact of the systems I build, whether that's through embedded software, machine learning, or hardware design. I'm excited to continue growing as an engineer and a person, and to see where this journey takes me next.
